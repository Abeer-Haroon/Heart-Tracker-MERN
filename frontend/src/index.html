Info

Heart failure is a major growing public health burden worldwide. One in 5 men and women will develop heart failure in their lifetime. In the United States alone, it is estimated that more than 8 million people will face heart failure by the year 2030, with projected direct medical costs doubling in the next 20 years to $53 billion, and similar trends are projected worldwide. A lot of time and resources are wasted on heart failure prediction and most of the time it is too late. Automating and simplifying this process using data science will help the patients and the doctors as well since doctors will spend more time on the prevention and treatment for the patient. The need for strategies to prevent heart failure has sharpened the focus on identifying and treating high-risk asymptomatic individuals.
